SSA Your PDGA Player Rating (PR) is a number that shows how close your average round scores are compared to the course rating, called the SSA (Scratch Scoring Averages, of the courses you’ve played in competition. Players who average the SSA on courses played will have a rating of 1000 and are considered “scratch players”. A player who averages scores lower than SSAs on course they’ve played will have a rating over 1000. HLA Average Length (meter) of Holes of a course 当該コースの平均ホール長(メートル)
TD The difficulty of the course increases simply by increasing the distance. TD (Technical Difficulty) is one of difficulties excluding the difficulty due to the distance, which is calculated according to the following equation; TD = SSA / HLA * 10; コースの難度は単純に距離を伸ばせば上がるが, その距離による難度を排した技術的な難度を表す指標として, 以下の方程式に従い算出した数値;
TD = SSA / HLA * 10;
